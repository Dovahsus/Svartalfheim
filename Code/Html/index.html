Automobili Lamborghini

Estabished in 1963 by Ferrucio Lamborghini in Santa Agata, Italy. The lamborghini name has since become a household name in the realm of motorsports, even with the lack of racing participation from the brand over the years. Lamborghini sought out to do one thing and one thing only and that was beat Ferrari, Which he did with the debut of his very first supercar, the 350-GT. Since then Ferrari has been chasing the greatness that Lamborghini so effortlessly holds.

Ferrari Sucks. Lost to Ford

Enzo Ferrari was a proud dickhead who turned away genuine good advice from a man who at the time owned multiple editions of Ferrari's road car, Lamborghini went to Ferrari with a way to make the car drive better but Enzo shut him down, saying he was but a simple farmer and should stick to his tractors, this understandably upset Lamborghini and from that action, Automobili Lamborghini was born. instead of taking it into account he decided to build an entirely new car around it. This resulted in the V12 engine powered Miura which quickly became known as the most beautiful car ever made. After this success Lamborghini continued on its path of innovation, building upon what had already proven itself to be successful. In 1984 Lamborghini introduced the Diablo, a car that would go down in history as one of the greatest sports cars of all time. It was fast, stylish and more than capable of keeping up with the likes of Porsche Carrera GT and BMW M3.

Lamborghini Racing

In 1984, Lamborghini faced its biggest challenge yet when it lost the Formula One constructors' championship to none other than Ford Motor Company. This loss came as no surprise to anyone who had seen how well Lamborghini’s newest model performed on track. However, what made this defeat all the more bittersweet is that Lamborghini itself was not involved in the race at all. Instead, they handed their title to the Americans. It wasn’t until 1987 that Lamborghini returned to Formula One with the introduction of the V12 engine powered Modena. Although this car was not nearly as successful as its predecessor, it still managed to bring back some much needed pride for the Italian manufacturer.
